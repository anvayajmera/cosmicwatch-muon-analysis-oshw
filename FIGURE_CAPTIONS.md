# Figure Captions (Draft)

## Main Figures

### Figure 1. Muon Count Rate vs Atmospheric Pressure
Scatter plot of 10-minute coincidence muon rate versus surface pressure across all runs, with a global log-linear fit. The fitted slope confirms a significant inverse barometric effect (`beta = 0.1294 %/hPa`, `p = 4.45e-08`).

### Figure 2. Muon Count Rate vs Time
Raw 10-minute coincidence rates shown across the full UTC timeline with a 3-hour moving mean. The plot shows low-frequency variability and run-to-run differences before atmospheric correction.

### Figure 3. Raw vs Atmosphere-Corrected Rate Anomaly
Session-wise smoothed anomaly traces (raw and corrected). Atmospheric correction suppresses part of the long-period drift and isolates residual variability for diurnal testing.

### Figure 4. Count Distribution vs Poisson Model
Histogram of muon counts per 10-minute bin overlaid with a Poisson expectation. The distribution is broadly consistent with counting-statistics limits relevant to small-amplitude periodic signals.

### Figure 5. Diurnal Fold After Atmospheric Correction
Atmosphere-corrected rates folded by local hour with SEM error bars and a fitted 24-hour harmonic. The fitted wave is suggestive but not statistically significant (`joint p = 0.114`).

### Figure A7. Cumulative Diurnal Test
