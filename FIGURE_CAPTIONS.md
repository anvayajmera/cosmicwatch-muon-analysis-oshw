# Figure Captions (Final Set)

### Figure 1. Muon Count Rate vs Atmospheric Pressure
Ten-minute coincidence muon rate plotted against surface pressure with a global trend fit. The fitted slope shows a significant inverse barometric effect (beta = 0.1181 %/hPa, p = 1.75e-10).

### Figure 2. Muon Count Rate vs Time
Raw ten-minute coincidence rates across the full UTC timeline with a moving trend. The plot shows low-frequency drift and run-to-run baseline differences before correction.

### Figure 3. Raw vs Atmosphere-Corrected Rate Anomaly
Session-wise anomaly traces before and after atmospheric correction. Correction suppresses part of slow environmental drift and isolates residual variability for diurnal testing.

### Figure 4. Count Distribution vs Poisson Model
Histogram of counts per ten-minute bin compared with a Poisson expectation. The distribution is broadly consistent with counting-statistics limits.

### Figure 5. Diurnal Fold After Atmospheric Correction
Atmosphere-corrected rates folded by local hour with uncertainty bars and fitted 24-hour harmonic. The 24-hour term is statistically significant (joint p = 0.0112) with small amplitude (~0.63% of mean rate).

### Figure 6. Outdoor Temperature vs Corrected Muon Rate
Pressure/session-corrected muon anomaly versus outdoor station temperature (KSMQ), with binned means and confidence intervals. The fitted trend is weak and not significant.

### Figure 7. One-Graph Analysis Summary
Single composite overview combining pressure dependence, corrected diurnal fold, significance progression over elapsed days, and week-based checkpoint trend. This figure summarizes the full analysis path and when significance emerged.
